What Is Restorative Dentistry?

Restorative dentistry focuses on repairing or replacing damaged or missing teeth to restore function, health, and appearance. Unlike cosmetic treatments that primarily enhance aesthetics, restorative procedures are essential for proper chewing, speaking, and preventing further oral health complications.

Why it matters: Missing or damaged teeth can lead to bone loss, shifting teeth, difficulty eating, and reduced confidence. Modern restorative dentistry offers solutions that look, feel, and function like natural teeth.

Our Restorative Treatments

Dental Implants

Dental implants are the gold standard for tooth replacement. A titanium post is surgically placed in the jawbone, where it fuses naturally over time. A custom crown is then attached, creating a permanent replacement that looks and functions like a natural tooth. Implants preserve bone density and prevent surrounding teeth from shifting. They typically last 20+ years with proper care.

Dental Crowns

Crowns are custom-made caps that cover a damaged tooth to restore its shape, size, strength, and appearance. We use high-quality ceramic and porcelain materials that match your natural tooth color exactly. Crowns are typically placed in two visits and can last 10-15 years.

Dental Bridges

A bridge fills the gap created by one or more missing teeth. It consists of artificial teeth anchored to adjacent natural teeth or implants. Bridges restore your smile, improve chewing ability, and prevent remaining teeth from drifting out of position.

The Implant Process

1

Consultation

We evaluate your oral health with 3D imaging to determine if implants are right for you.

2

Implant Placement

The titanium post is surgically placed in your jawbone under local anesthesia.

3

Healing

The implant fuses with your bone over 3-6 months. A temporary restoration protects the site.

4

Final Restoration

A custom crown is attached to the implant. Your new tooth looks and functions naturally.
