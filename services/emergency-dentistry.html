What Is a Dental Emergency?

A dental emergency involves severe pain, bleeding, infection, or trauma that requires immediate professional attention. Ignoring these symptoms can lead to more serious complications, including tooth loss and systemic infection.

Common Dental Emergencies We Treat

  • Severe toothache that does not subside with over-the-counter pain relievers
  • Chipped, cracked, or broken teeth from accidents or biting hard objects
  • Knocked-out teeth from sports injuries or falls
  • Swelling or abscess indicating infection
  • Lost fillings or crowns causing sensitivity or pain
  • Broken orthodontic appliances causing discomfort
  • Bleeding gums or soft tissue injuries that do not stop

What to Do in a Dental Emergency

1

Stay Calm

Take a deep breath. Most dental emergencies are treatable and we are here to help.

2

Call Us Immediately

Call (310) 645-2886 and describe your symptoms. Our team will advise on immediate steps and prepare for your arrival.

3

Manage Pain & Bleeding

Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling. Use gauze for bleeding. Avoid aspirin as it can increase bleeding.

4

Come In

Visit our office for prompt evaluation and treatment. We prioritize emergency patients.
